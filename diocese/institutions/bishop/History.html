A Brief Historical Sketch

Journey of Faith in the Diocese of Warangal

Key Historical Milestones

  • 52 A.D. – St. Thomas the Apostle arrived in India, preached the Gospel in Kerala, worked in Mylapore (Chennai), and was martyred.
  • 1498 – Vasco da Gama’s arrival brought Portuguese missionaries to India.
  • 1510 – Fr. Luis do Salvador, a Franciscan missionary in Vijayanagar, was killed, becoming the First Martyr of the Church in Andhra.
  • 1689 – French Jesuits started the Carnatic Mission, strengthening the Christian presence in Andhra.
  • 1776 – After Jesuit suppression, the mission was entrusted to the Paris Foreign Mission Society (MEP).
  • 1832 – Madras erected as a Vicariate, covering the entire Telugu mission.
  • 1850 – Visakhapatnam Vicariate created under MSFS missionaries.
  • 1851 – Hyderabad Vicariate established, entrusted to PIME missionaries of Milan.
  • 1886 – Hyderabad raised to a diocese, strengthening the mission in Telangana.
  • 1933 – Vijayawada separated from Hyderabad as a Sui Iuris mission.
  • 1937 – Vijayawada officially erected as a diocese.

Through centuries of faith, sacrifice, and missionary zeal, the Diocese of Warangal stands today as a living witness to the Gospel in Telangana.
